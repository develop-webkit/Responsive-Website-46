†Pre-Qualified Credit Offer:

You received this offer because you meet lender’s initial credit worthiness criteria. If your application information differs from the information used during your pre-qualification or if you are unable to meet lender’s full credit eligibility criteria, your application may not be approved. We did not know your income during the pre-qualification process, so it was not considered when determining pre-qualification status. You will be asked to provide your income when you apply and if it does not meet our minimum requirements, your application may not be approved. We also did not know your housing expenses, which will be considered during the application process and may impact your approval. This letter is not a loan approval, nor is it a guarantee that you will receive a loan through LendingUSA. In order to receive a final loan approval, you must enroll in a participating program and pay the Down Payment*; submit a formal application; and pass our final underwriting review, which is inclusive of: (a) your continuing to meet criteria for this prescreened offer; (b) your credit report, application and credit history meeting our pre-established credit criteria, including maintaining a minimum 500 FICO score; (c) your monthly debt-to-income ratio (i.e., total monthly debt payments including rent an mortgage divided by monthly gross income) not exceeding 70%; and (d) verification of your identity, income and/or employment. An origination fee of up to 8% may be included in the principal loan amount that may result in an APR of up to 29.99%. Amounts financed range from $1,000 to $35,000 but varies by state. Loans are not available in all states. Loan proceeds shall only be used to pay off and consolidate the accounts that you enrolled in the participating program and must be repaid.

*Participating Program Overview:

Upon contacting LendingUSA, you will be referred to a participating program provider (“PPP”) that offers a participating program. LendingUSA may receive compensation from the PPP related to this referral. By enrolling in a participating program, the PPP will create a payment plan for you and set up a customer dedicated account for you to make those payments to, so that they can be escrowed. You will then be asked to stop paying on your enrolled accounts. The PPP will then attempt to negotiate your enrolled accounts down to less than what you currently owe and use those escrowed payments to pay off your creditors over time for a fee. Enrolling and participating in a participating program may have adverse credit, tax, and legal consequences. To be eligible to apply for a Freshstart loan, you must enroll in a PPP program and deposit a specified percentage of your combined enrolled account total into your customer dedicated account as a down payment (“Down Payment”).
